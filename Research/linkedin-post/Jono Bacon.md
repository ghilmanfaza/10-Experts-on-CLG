Your content marketing strategy probably isn't broken...your execution is just isn't that great.

We've watched countless tech companies abandon content after three blog posts and 56 views (12 of which were the team checking if the links worked), then declare: "Content doesn't work for us. Our audience is different."

No. Your audience isn't different...

...you expected results in three weeks from a strategy that takes six to twelve months to even start breathing. That's not a strategy failure... that's execution wearing a strategy costume.

Here's what the data actually shows:

 * Only 22% of B2B marketers rate their content marketing as "extremely successful." And the biggest differentiator? The successful ones have a documented strategy AND they stick with it long enough to matter.

 * Meanwhile, 70% of B2B content never even gets used because it misses buyer intent entirely.

The pattern we've seen repeatedly: companies try to do everything at once. Blog. Podcast. YouTube. Discord. Newsletter. Meetups. Ambassador program.

Congratulations. You've achieved the worst possible outcome... being everywhere but not resonating in a meaningful way.

The fix isn't sexy. It's actually rather boring:

Pick one channel. Do it properly. Measure what matters (intent, not vanity metrics). Give it actual time to work before declaring it dead.

Content marketing isn't magic. It's compound interest for trust and we drive results with (boring) iteration.
