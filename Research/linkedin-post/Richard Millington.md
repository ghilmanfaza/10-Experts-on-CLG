### [Linkedin post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richard-millington-5a32782_im-quite-excited-about-the-value-of-community-activity-7448010615056424962-mPGu?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAACEyXl8BODt9B5rGcJ4HIqqlyDY_7cSqa6Y)

I'm quite excited about the value of Community Knowledge in this new AI-driven world we're entering. 

It seems to require a different sort of mindset. 

For a long time, our focus has been on making it easier to engage in communities. 

This meant reducing friction, removing barriers, and encouraging as many contributions as possible. 

The future, I suspect, will be about making it easier to make QUALITY contributions. 

This means adding friction, barriers, and filtering good from the bad. 

This will probably be less about measuring the quantity of members, contributions, and activity, and more about measuring the quality of contributions. 

A different kind of problem, but a fun one to explore.
