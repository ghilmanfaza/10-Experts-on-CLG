The biggest mistake I see in community building: Thinking you need hundreds of active members to be successful. 

You actually need 10. Just 10.

That scares a lot of people I work with because it feels *too small.* But as strategist adrienne maree brown says, "Small is all." I've seen it again and again and again. Get this wrong, and things will feel inexplicably off for months or years to come. 

Those 10 people become the foundation for everything that follows. They're your founding members, and they help you: 

- Create the early content and conversations
- Model the culture and behavior you want to see
- Welcome new members and make them feel like they belong

And here's what most community builders miss: In EVERY successful community, 4-20% of members provide most of the value. That small core is where you focus your energy.

The math is simple: Start with 10 → Grow to 50 → Now you have a core of 10-15 active leaders. Then keep going. 

Of course, those leaders don't just emerge organically. You need to:

💻 Hand-pick or invite applications
👋🏼 Onboard them intentionally in cohorts
🥰 Monitor workload and recognize contributions
🚫 Let go of people who take more than they give

More isn't always better. A small, engaged core beats a large, passive crowd every. single. time.
