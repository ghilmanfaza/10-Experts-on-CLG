Community tools shouldn't work in competition, rather they should seek to exist in cohesive collaboration and to work towards a beautiful community flywheel.

It's not either forum or chat space. It's both together, in cohesion. They are good at different things.

It's not a choice between social media or forum. It's both together and with creativity.

It's not email or community platform. It's both with effectiveness and respect of people's habits.

It's not in person or online. It's what the community organisers and people have capacity for.

A decline, for example, in enterprise forums, is not necessarily because of the forum itself. It's because they tend to exist in isolation. Build a community flywheel, work towards integrating and valuing of the tools that are used. 

You can use inspiration, content and ideas from social media to bring into your forum efforts.

Or you can get creative with your emails to help boost attention to forum discussions.

Sometimes tools don't really serve you, and it's ok to show them the door. There's been a lot of that happening in the past couple of years. Often there are too many tools to manage and we have to focus on the ones that make most impact.
